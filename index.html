Our mission

We seek to empower underprivileged youth by giving them access to creativity, music, instruments, and therapy. We believe that music has the power to inspire, heal, and unite members of a community from all walks of life. The Backstage Pass Project exists to open doors so kids who might never have the chance to hold a guitar, write a song, or sit front row at a concert can discover their own rhythm and voice — because when a young person finds their sound, they find themselves.

Vision

We envision a world where music is accessible to every young person and serves as a tool for personal growth, emotional wellness, and community connection.

Community

Through inclusive programming and youth-driven events, we build a creative ecosystem that inspires long-term confidence, belonging, and opportunity.

What makes us different

We don’t just teach music — we create experiences, and we are youth-led and created. Concerts, open-mic nights, workshops, school partnerships, and hands-on projects let youth step into the spotlight, feel valued, and connect with peers.

Core values

Our work is guided by five values that show up in every program.

Access

Every young person deserves the chance to experience music — no exceptions.

Creativity

We encourage artistic freedom, experimentation, and self-expression.

Opportunity

We create pathways for youth to explore music, develop skills, and access meaningful experiences that can shape their futures.

Empowerment

We lift up youth voices and give them platforms to succeed.

Community

Music brings people together, and so do we.

Healing

We believe in the emotional and restorative power of music.

Why music matters

Music is not just entertainment; it is energy, emotion, and expression that helps young people process trauma, build confidence, and connect in ways words cannot.

Stronger learning

Youth engaged in music show better memory, stronger language skills, improved focus, and higher academic outcomes.

Social & emotional growth

Music builds collaboration, discipline, confidence, and emotional regulation, leading to stronger peer connections and communication skills.

Health & resilience

Regular engagement reduces stress, supports mood, and offers a healthy outlet for processing emotions and building resilience.

What we do

We expose and introduce underprivileged youth to the arts through community programs, workshops, and concerts, creating spaces where young people can explore their creativity, connect with others, and heal through sound and expression.

Music therapy sessions

Guided programs that use sound and rhythm for emotional healing and self-expression.

Instrument donation drives

Collecting and distributing gently used instruments to kids who cannot afford them.

Workshops & creative labs

Hands-on experiences with local musicians, producers, and artists.

Community concerts

Bringing youth, schools, and performers together to share the stage and the story.

Our story

What started as a small movement in Cleveland is growing into a vision for a connected network of musicians, schools, and communities united by the transformative power of music. We have seen the spark in kids who hit their first chord or hear live music for the first time. The Backstage Pass Project is led by co-board directors Casie Baker, Chase Takaoka, and Colson Baker — three people from different worlds who share one belief: music saves lives.

Our progress so far

Even as a new organization, our momentum is undeniable.

Our vision

We envision a world where creativity is not a privilege — it is a pathway. The Backstage Pass Project will grow into an international network that unites schools, artists, and communities across borders, from Cleveland to the world, from classrooms to concert halls. Through music, we aim to make an impact that lasts far beyond the stage.
